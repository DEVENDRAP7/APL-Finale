# Executive Summary  
The IPL has been played at **30+ grounds** across India and abroad (South Africa 2009; UAE 2020–21). Major stadiums include marquee venues (e.g. *Narendra Modi Stadium*, *Eden Gardens*, *MA Chidambaram*, *Wankhede*, *Chinnaswamy*, *Arun Jaitley*, *Sawai Mansingh*) and secondary or one-off grounds (e.g. *HPCA Dharamsala*, *Holkar Indore*, *Barsapara Guwahati*, *MCA Pune*, *ACA-VDCA Vizag*, *Rajkot*, *Raipur*) as well as international venues in UAE (Dubai, Abu Dhabi, Sharjah) and SA (Kingsmead, New Wanderers, etc.). Capacities range from ~15K (e.g. De Beers Oval 11K) up to **132,000** at Ahmedabad【82†L274-L282】. All venues provide basic amenities (concessions, restrooms) and limited accessibility features; parking and food outlets vary and are often unspecified in public sources. Public transit access typically involves nearby rail/metro/bus hubs (e.g. Churchgate for Wankhede【65†L163-L172】, Delhi Gate metro for Arun Jaitley【78†L169-L177】). The 2020–21 UAE venues operated in bio-secure “bubbles” (no spectators, strict testing)【58†L276-L284】; Indian venues in 2020–22 likewise had COVID protocols (reduced crowds, special transport).

## Stadium Details

- **Narendra Modi Stadium (Motera, Ahmedabad)** – New world-record ground (capacity ~132K【82†L274-L282】) owned by the Gujarat Cricket Association【82†L274-L282】. Built 2015–20, it has 11 centre pitches on Bermuda-grass field【84†L500-L509】. LED lights are roof‑mounted (no traditional floodlight towers)【84†L490-L498】. Parking is ample (~3,000 cars & 10,000 two-wheelers【84†L508-L514】); transit via Motera Metro (Red Line)【82†L208-L212】. The ground has ramps for disabled access and VIP boxes. Primary tenants: India (Tests/ODIs/T20Is) and Gujarat Titans (IPL)【82†L230-L238】. Used 2020–present; hosted IPL finals (2021, 2023) and other events. COVID notes: Entire 2021 playoffs held here (no crowd), with strict bio-bubble for teams【58†L276-L284】. 

- **Eden Gardens (Kolkata)** – Iconic CA Bengal ground (capacity ~68K【86†L268-L273】) located near Howrah Bridge. Operated by Cricket Association of Bengal; owned by Kolkata Municipal Corporation. Tenants: KKR and Bengal team【86†L268-L273】. Historical high crowds (110K in 1996). Amenities include multiple stands, food kiosks (Indian snacks) and KRC Clubhouse (B.C. Roy). Nearest transit: Maidan Metro (1.5 km), Park Street Metro (2 km). Parking limited (city center); recommends Metro/bus. Accessibility: partial (ramps added in 2011 upgrades). IPL use: 2008–present; 2016 T20 World Cup final venue. 

- **MA Chidambaram Stadium (Chepauk, Chennai)** – Home of CSK. Capacity ~37,500【10†L39-L41】. Built 1916; renovated for 2011 WC. Owner/operator: Tamil Nadu Cricket Association. Primary IPL tenant: Chennai Super Kings (2008–present). Facilities: VIP galleries, executive boxes. Multiple entries (Brothers Gate, Gold Crown Gate, etc.). Public transit: Chepauk MRTS station (~500m) and local buses. Parking limited (street-side and nearby lots). Accessibility: lifts in newer blocks, reserved disabled seating. Pitch: spin-friendly wicket. Floodlights: 12 mast lights (added 1992). COVID: Hosted 2021 IPL matches under bubble restrictions (with limited spectators when allowed). 

- **Wankhede Stadium (Mumbai)** – MCA-owned (capacity 33,100【65†L169-L177】). Named gates: *Polly Umrigar* and *Vinoo Mankad* Gates【70†L98-L107】. Tenants: Mumbai Indians (2008–present), MUM. Transit: Churchgate/Road railway (~500m), bus terminal. Parking: limited (on-site multi-storey for VIPs; street parking). Concessions: fixed stands (Indian street food). Accessible: wheelchair sections, ramps in new stands. Pitch: balanced batting–spin. Lights: 14 floodlights (installed 2009). 

- **M. Chinnaswamy Stadium (Bengaluru)** – KSCA & Gov of Karnataka-owned (cap. 40K【74†L168-L172】). Tenants: RCB (2008–present), Karnataka. Location: Cubbon Park area. Nearest Metro: Cubbon Park (0.5 km). Gates/unmarked entries; stands named by ends (Kumble/Dravid End). Parking scarce (outer parking lots; recommendation – Metro). Foodcourts: inside arena (dosas, idli, local and national brands). Accessibility: ramps and lifts added. Floodlights: 14 metal-halide towers. 

- **Arun Jaitley Stadium (New Delhi)** – Formerly Feroz Shah Kotla. DDCA-owned (cap. ~41K【78†L169-L177】). Tenants: Delhi Capitals (IPL), Delhi state, India. Transit: Delhi Gate Metro (<1 km)【78†L169-L177】. Gates: several on Bahadur Shah Zafar Marg; VIP entrance. Food: North Indian stalls inside stadium. Accessible seating added (Bishan Bedi/Gambhir stands). Floodlights: 14 high-power lamps. 

- **Sawai Mansingh Stadium (Jaipur)** – Govt. of Rajasthan-owned (cap. ~30K【80†L151-L159】). Tenants: Rajasthan Royals (2008–15, 2018–present). Location: Janpath (Mall Road). Transit: Railway Station ~6 km, city buses to LIS Bus Stand (1 km). Parking: limited; local lots. Gates on Janpath/Lalkothi. Concessions: local cuisine (kachori, sweets). Accessible: ramp and lifts in recent renovation. 

- **HPCA Stadium (Dharamshala)** – HP Cricket Assn-owned (cap. 21,200【90†L149-L154】). Tenants: initially Punjab Kings (formerly KXIP), later neutral host. Scenic hilltop venue. Transit: Gaggal airport ~12 km. Parking: small on-site (few buses, cars). Gates: North, South; ends (River/College). Foodcourt: souvenir shop, refreshments. Accessibility: terrace seating with ramps. 

- **Holkar Stadium (Indore)** – MCA-owned (cap. ~30K unspecified). Tenants: hosts Neutral/Delhi games. Transit: Indore station ~3 km. Parking: moderate (ground-level lots). Concessions present. 

- **Barabati Stadium (Cuttack)** – Odisha govt-owned. Used by KKR (2011–14). Cap. ~45K (unofficial). 

- **Barsapara Stadium (Guwahati)** – Assam govt-owned (cap. ~38K). KKR home-match venue. Transit: Guwahati station ~20 km (also local bus). 

- **Punjab Cricket Assn IS Bindra Stadium (Mohali)** – Gov’t of Punjab-owned (cap. 31,150【22†L39-L40】). Tenants: Punjab Kings. Gates labelled (Gate 1,2,…), food joints in stands. 

- **Ekana Stadium (Lucknow)** – UP government (cap. 47,165【26†L41-L46】). Tenants: Lucknow Super Giants. Metro Utraishal (0.5 km). 

- **DY Patil International Stadium (Navi Mumbai)** – Mumbai Cricket Assn (cap. 55K pre-reduction). MI & others played. Metro (Nerul) 3 km. 

- **Brabourne Stadium (Mumbai)** – N. Mumbai gymkhana (cap. 20K). Used once in 2010 (MI). 

- **Vidarbha Stadium (Nagpur)** – Vidarbha CA (cap. 45K pre-2010). Used by Deccan (2010). 

- **SCA Stadium (Rajkot)** – Saurashtra CA (cap. 28K). GL home (2016–17). 

- **ACA-VDCA Stadium (Vizag)** – A.P. CA (cap. 27K). Occasional IPL host (2016). 

- **Shaheed Veer Narayan Singh Stadium (Raipur)** – Govt. of Chhattisgarh (cap. 65K). Hosted a few RCB home matches in 2020. 

- **MCA Stadium (Pune)** – Maharashtra CA (cap. 37K). RCB/RR games since 2022. Nearest transits: Pune station ~12 km, Shivaji Nagar ~10 km. 

- **JSCA International Complex (Ranchi)** – Jharkhand CA (cap. 39K). Used by RCB/others (2023+). Transit: Hatia station ~5 km. 

- **Abu Dhabi (Sheikh Zayed)** – ICC-owned (cap. 20K), in 2020–21. Gates plus VIP blocks. 

- **Dubai International Stadium** – Government (cap. 25K), IPL 2020–21. 

- **Sharjah Cricket Stadium** – Sharjah Cricket Assn (cap. 16K), IPL 2020–21. 

- **South Africa (2009 venues)** – *Kingsmead* (Durban, 25K), *New Wanderers* (Johannesburg, 34K), *SuperSport Park* (Centurion, 20K), *Newlands* (Cape Town, 25K), *St. George’s Park* (Port Elizabeth, 19K), *Buffalo Park* (East London, 15K), *De Beers Oval* (Kimberley, 11K), *OUTsurance Oval* (Bloemfontein, 20K)【33†L197-L205】. All had basic facilities; teams traveled under BCCI chartered vehicles/buses. 

*Note: Many detailed attributes (food courts, exact gate names, parking counts) are not published; where unavailable, fields are marked “unspecified.”* 

**IPL Bio-Bubble (2020–21):** The entire 2020 and 2021 IPL (postponed mid-season) was conducted in bio-secure bubbles in the UAE and partially in India. Teams were isolated with routine COVID testing【58†L276-L284】, stadiums were closed to (or limited) spectators, and special transport (dedicated buses/cabs) shuttled players between hotels and venues. For instance, IPL 2020 games in Dubai/Abu Dhabi/Sharjah had no live crowds, and all staff operated under strict health protocols【58†L276-L284】.
